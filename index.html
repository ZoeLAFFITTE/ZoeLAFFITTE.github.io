Team: Methods and Computational Anatomy (MeCA)

I am a PhD student at the Institut de Neurosciences de la Timone (INT) in Marseille, France. My research focuses on the spatial organisation and co-variation of cortical folds during human neonatal brain development. More specifically, I aim to identify conserved and reproducible sulcal landmarks and model their developmental trajectories. These landmarks provide robust references for studying cortical maturation in healthy and pathological contexts and contribute to defining quantitative markers of brain development. I use geometric and topological approaches on 3D-reconstructed cortical surfaces derived from MRI data.

OHBM 2026

Summary The MeCA team had the opportunity to attend the 32nd Annual Meeting of the Organization for Human Brain Mapping (OHBM), held in Bordeaux, France, from June 14 to June 18, 2026. I presented a poster based on my Master’s final-year internship project, which focused on improving neonatal cortical surface registration. This work studied registration failures related to default MSM parameter settings and proposed optimized parameters for better alignment of neonatal brain surfaces. The optimized parameters and code are available on GitHub.
